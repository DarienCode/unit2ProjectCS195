Evolution of Solar Panels

1839 - Discovery of the Photovoltaic Effect

French physicist Alexandre Edmond Becquerel discovered the photovoltaic effect, which laid the foundation for modern solar technology.

1954 - First Silicon Solar Cell

Bell Labs created the first silicon-based solar cell with 6% efficiency, marking a key milestone in solar technology.

1958 - Solar Panels in Space

NASA used solar panels to power the Vanguard 1 satellite, the first use of solar power in space exploration.

1980 - First Solar Cell Exceeding 20% Efficiency

The University of New South Wales developed a solar cell exceeding 20% efficiency, pushing solar technology forward.

2000s - Thin-Film Solar Panels

Thin-film solar panels became commercially viable, using less material and offering more flexible applications.

2010s - Perovskite Solar Cells

Perovskite solar cells offered high efficiency at a lower cost, with the potential to surpass 30% efficiency in the future.
