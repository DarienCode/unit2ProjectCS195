Solar Panel Innovation

Solar technology has evolved significantly over the past century, driven by breakthroughs like the discovery of the photovoltaic effect in 1839 and the development of the first silicon solar cell in 1954. Early milestones, such as NASA's use of solar panels in space, paved the way for global adoption. Efficiency improvements, like the 20% mark reached in the 1980s, made solar energy more viable. Thin-film technologies in the 2000s offered flexible, cost-effective alternatives. Recently, perovskite and tandem cells have revolutionized the field, with efficiency records approaching 30%, showing immense potential for the future of renewable energy.
